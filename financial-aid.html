Financial Support Programs & Sponsors

We work with multiple organizations to help you fund your education

Funding Your Education

Alent Institute does not offer its own financial aid program. However, we honor grants and scholarships awarded to our students from several state and community organizations. Many of our students receive full or partial funding through these programs.

Our admissions team can help you explore the financial support options available to you. We are committed to making quality healthcare education accessible to all qualified students.

Financial Support Sources

Organizations that sponsor our students

One Stop Career Centers

New Jersey's One Stop Career Centers provide career services, training resources, and financial assistance for eligible individuals seeking vocational training and employment.

Division of Vocational Rehabilitation (DVR)

The DVR helps individuals with disabilities prepare for, obtain, and maintain employment through vocational rehabilitation services, including funding for training programs.

Workforce New Jersey

Workforce NJ provides funding and support for job training programs to help New Jersey residents develop the skills needed for in-demand careers in healthcare and other industries.

Essex County Division of Training & Employment

Provides workforce development services and training grants to Essex County residents seeking to enter or advance in the healthcare field.

Newark Works

Newark Works connects city residents with job training opportunities and provides financial support for approved vocational programs including healthcare training.

Mayor's Office of Employment & Training (MOET)

Provides employment and training services to city residents, including funding for vocational education programs at approved institutions.

Trade Adjustment Assistance (TRA/TAA)

Federal program that provides assistance to workers who have lost their jobs due to foreign trade, including funding for retraining in new career fields like healthcare.

Workforce Innovation & Opportunity Act (WIOA)

Federal workforce development program that funds job training for eligible adults, dislocated workers, and youth seeking careers in high-demand industries.

Veterans Educational Benefits

Veterans and eligible dependents may use their educational benefits to fund training at Alent Institute. We support various VA education programs.

Catholic Charities

Provides financial assistance for vocational training to eligible individuals through their various social services and workforce development programs.

How to Apply for Financial Support

Follow these steps to explore financial support options for your training at Alent Institute:

  • Contact our admissions office to discuss your financial situation and eligibility
  • Visit your local One Stop Career Center to learn about available programs
  • Contact the Division of Vocational Rehabilitation if you have a qualifying disability
  • Check with your county's workforce development board for training grants
  • If you are a veteran, contact your local VA office about education benefits
  • Bring any approval letters or vouchers to your admissions interview

Need help? Our admissions team is experienced in working with these organizations and can guide you through the process. Call us at (973) 555-0140 (Newark) or (201) 555-0180 (Jersey City) to get started.
